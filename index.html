What is Masters Boxing?

Welcome to the USA Elite Masters Boxing website. Our goal is to put on USA Masters Boxing events that are streamlined, professional, and creating a pleasant competitive environment. So what is Masters Boxing? and who can compete in this division? Masters boxing is an amateur boxing division sanctioned by USA Boxing. To compete you must be 35 years of age or older, have never competed in any combat sport as a professional, and must be registered with an up to date USA Boxing Masters passbook and yearly physical. Those that are 45 years of age or older must also pass an exercise EKG also known as a stress test (the ekg is valid for 5 years). The process can sometimes be a challenge so we built this website to help guide you and hopefully answer any questions you might have. For a step by step guide on how to get your yellow USA Boxing Masters passbook, click on the link below.

How to Get Your Yellow USA Boxing Masters Passbook

Follow the registration process at www.usaboxing.com.

To compete as a master boxer you must be 35 years of age or older, never competed as a pro in any combat sport, and you must be registered with USA Boxing and be up to date with your Yellow Masters Passbook as well as your yearly physical (Those ages 45 and older must also pass an exercise EKG also known as a stress test and is good for 5 years). Your first step is to download the masters medical form from the link below. This is the form you will have your doctor fill out and clear you as fit to box in order to be eligible to compete. Once you have your medical form done and cleared as fit to box, the next step is register with USA Boxing as Master Boxer. Click on the Register with USA Boxing link below, if you are a former member and need to renew, click the log-in and on the sidebar click on the renew membership button. For those that are new and none members, you want to click on the Join USA Boxing button. Take your time and follow the instructions, when you get to what type of membership are you signing up for make sure you select athlete and master boxer. Complete your registration, complete your safesport training. At the end of your registration, it will let you know who you need to send the following to…. A copy of your birth certificate or travel passport, 2 passport size photos of you, a copy of your passed physical. It normally takes 2 to 3 weeks for your LBC to send you your passbook. Once your membership with USA Boxing is active, you also want to upload your birth certificate or passport and the second page of your physical form that clears you as fit to box. and your profile picture. Once this is complete and you have your yellow passbook, you are official and ready to compete as a master boxer.

EQUIPMENT

Only gloves are provided by USA Boxing, all other gear is your responsibility. Headgear must be a USA Boxing Masters approved headgear. Title, Ringside, Sting are the only 3 companies that are approved. You can use tape and gauze or traditional handwraps, you can not mix gauze and tape with traditional handwraps. You must use one or the other.

USA BOXERS & COACHES

you must be up-to-date with your membership with USA Boxing. Boxers must also have their medical up-to- date and yellow masters book with them in order to compete. Coaches must be up-to-date with their USA Boxing membership and passed the Green Level Coaching Test and Safesport. You’ll need to have your redbook that USA Boxing gives out to coaches. Without completion, you will not be able to corner your boxer. No exceptions will be made.

INTERNATIONAL BOXERS & COACHES

You must be in good standing and up-to-date with your Countries Federation’s membership Boxers must also have an up-to-date physical with those 45 years of age or older also having a passed exercise EKG with in the past 5 years. You must have your up-to-date card or book from your federation with you.

BOXERS WITHOUT COACHES

If you are traveling without a coach we will have coaches that will be ready to help corner you for your bout.
